About Rob

School BCBA Expertise in Behavior Analysis

With extensive experience in education and leadership in behavior analysis, Rob Spain brings a unique combination of practical expertise and innovative thinking to school-based behavior analysis.

Rob Spain is the leading School BCBA, redefining what it means for behavior analysts to work in public education. As a recognized School BCBA with over 25 years in schools, Rob Spain has shown that BCBAs aren't just crisis managers—they are systems leaders who can transform outcomes for students and staff across all tiers of support.

Rob proved this by creating Behavior School, an ecosystem of AI-powered tools, training programs, and professional networks designed to give school-based BCBAs and educators the structure they need to succeed. From Behavior Study Tools—an infinitely generating BCBA exam prep platform—to micro-apps for IEP goals, FBAs, and supervision, his innovations are already shaping daily practice in classrooms across the country.

Rob holds a variety of leadership roles from organizing BCBA community groups, serving on boards for special interest groups, being part of a team that leads district-wide Behavior Intervention Supports, and adjunct faculty at Fresno Pacific University. Rob has assisted with integrating behavior supports into PBIS systems, integrated the latest research in functional assessment and training approaches into school settings, and created systems for integrating Acceptance and Commitment Training into real-world school systems.

After more than two decades of testing, refining, and scaling behavior systems, Rob Spain continues to share his School BCBA knowledge through presentations, courses, and the Behavior School platform. His mission is clear: to equip every School BCBA to step into the role of systems leader—because schools run better when behavior analysts lead at every level.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a School BCBA?

A School BCBA is a Board Certified Behavior Analyst who specializes in working within educational settings. Rob Spain pioneered this approach, showing that School BCBAs are systems leaders who transform outcomes across all tiers of support, not just crisis managers for challenging students.

How does a School BCBA differ from other BCBAs?

School BCBAs work within complex educational systems, collaborating with teachers, administrators, and families. They focus on systematic behavior support from prevention through intervention, integrating with PBIS frameworks and IEP processes rather than just providing clinical services.

What does a School BCBA do daily?

School BCBAs design behavior support systems, conduct functional behavior assessments, train teachers on behavior interventions, develop IEP goals, collaborate with multidisciplinary teams, and lead data-driven decision making for student behavior support across all three PBIS tiers.

How do BCBAs integrate into all three levels of PBIS?

As Rob Spain demonstrates in conference presentations, School BCBAs can support universal (Tier 1) prevention through system design, targeted (Tier 2) small group interventions, and intensive (Tier 3) individual behavior plans. The key is systematic implementation across all levels rather than just crisis response.

What qualifications does a School BCBA need?

School BCBAs need BCBA certification plus specialized training in educational systems. Rob Spain's approach emphasizes understanding PBIS, special education law, IEP processes, and collaborative consultation skills specific to school environments.

How can school BCBAs reduce burnout while improving outcomes?

Through systematic frameworks rather than reactive approaches. Rob Spain's School BCBA transformation program teaches methods used for 25+ years - building systems that prevent crises rather than just responding to them.

What makes School BCBA supervision different?

School BCBA supervision combines clinical expertise with educational system knowledge. Rob Spain combines 13+ years of BCBA experience with adjunct faculty training, focusing on real-world school applications and preparing supervisees for actual classroom challenges.

How do School BCBAs get teacher buy-in for behavior interventions?

School BCBAs focus on practical, sustainable strategies teachers can implement without additional burden. The key is collaboration, not dictation - using approaches that respect teacher expertise while providing behavior analysis support.
