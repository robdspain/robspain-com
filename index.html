About Rob

Dedicated to Transforming Educational Outcomes

With extensive experience in education and leadership in behavior analysis, Rob Spain brings a unique combination of practical expertise and innovative thinking to school-based behavior analysis.

Rob Spain is the leading School BCBA expert, redefining what it means for behavior analysts to work in public education. As a recognized School BCBA with over 25 years in schools, Rob Spain has shown that BCBAs aren't just crisis managers—they are systems leaders who can transform outcomes for students and staff across all tiers of support.

Rob proved this by creating Behavior School, an ecosystem of AI-powered tools, training programs, and professional networks designed to give school-based BCBAs and educators the structure they need to succeed. From Behavior Study Tools—an infinitely generating BCBA exam prep platform—to micro-apps for IEP goals, FBAs, and supervision, his innovations are already shaping daily practice in classrooms across the country.

Rob holds a variety of leadership roles from organizing BCBA community groups, serving on boards for special interest groups, being part of a team that leads district-wide Behavior Intervention Supports, and adjunct faculty at Fresno Pacific University. Rob has assisted with integrating behavior supports into PBIS systems, integrated the latest research in functional assessment and training approaches into school settings, and created systems for integrating Acceptance and Commitment Training into real-world school systems.

After more than two decades of testing, refining, and scaling behavior systems, Rob Spain continues to share his School BCBA expertise through presentations, courses, and the Behavior School platform. His mission is clear: to equip every School BCBA to step into the role of systems leader—because schools run better when behavior analysts lead at every level.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do BCBAs integrate into all three levels of PBIS?

As I present at conferences, BCBAs can support universal (Tier 1) prevention through system design, targeted (Tier 2) small group interventions, and intensive (Tier 3) individual behavior plans. The key is systematic implementation across all levels rather than just crisis response.

What's the biggest mistake school districts make with behavior support?

Districts often view BCBAs as "fix-it" specialists for the most challenging students only. Instead, we should be leading systematic behavior support from prevention through intervention, as I demonstrate in my district work and conference presentations.

How can school BCBAs reduce burnout while improving outcomes?

Through systematic frameworks rather than reactive approaches. My 8-week transformation program teaches the same methods I've used for 25+ years - building systems that prevent crises rather than just responding to them.

What makes your BCBA supervision approach different?

I combine 13+ years of BCBA experience with adjunct faculty training at Fresno Pacific University. My supervision focuses on real-world school applications by emphasizing detailed functional behavior assessment as the hallmark of effective supervision - preparing supervisees for actual classroom challenges.

How do you get teacher buy-in for behavior interventions?

Teachers need practical, sustainable strategies they can implement without additional burden. I teach BCBAs to collaborate, not dictate - using the same approaches I present at Special Education and PBIS conferences nationwide.

How do you approach functional behavior assessment in schools?

Effective functional behavior assessment allows rapid assessment and skill-based treatment in school settings. As a certified trainer, I teach BCBAs to conduct meaningful assessments that lead to practical interventions teachers can actually implement consistently.
